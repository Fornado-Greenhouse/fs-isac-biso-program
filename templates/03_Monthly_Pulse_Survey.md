# Monthly Pulse Survey Template

**Purpose:** Quick monthly check on stakeholder satisfaction with BISO partnership
**Time to Complete:** <2 minutes for stakeholders, 10 minutes/month to analyze
**Frequency:** Monthly
**Primary Use:** Early warning system for relationship health issues

---

## What is the Monthly Pulse Survey?

A single-question monthly survey that tracks stakeholder satisfaction trends. This is your early warning system ‚Äî if satisfaction drops, you know to investigate before trust erodes.

**Key Principle:** Keep it simple. One question + optional comment = high response rates.

**Target:** >4.0/5.0 average across all stakeholders

## Implementation Guide

### Month 1: Set Up Your Survey

**Choose Your Method:**
- ‚úÖ **Email (simplest):** Just send personalized emails monthly
- ‚úÖ **Survey tool (scalable):** Google Forms, SurveyMonkey, Typeform
- ‚úÖ **Slack/Teams (digital orgs):** Native reactions + comments
- ‚úÖ **Hybrid:** Email link to web form

**Set Up Tracking Spreadsheet:**
```
File: BISO_Monthly_Pulse_Tracking.xlsx or .csv

| Month | Stakeholder | Business Unit | Score (1-5) | Comment | Follow-Up Needed |
|-------|-------------|---------------|-------------|---------|------------------|
| Jan   | VP Retail   | Retail        | 4           | None    | No               |
| Jan   | CFO         | Finance       | 3           | "Slow response times" | Yes - schedule call |
```

### Month 2+: Build Your Rhythm

**Timing:** Send survey on the same day each month (e.g., first Monday)

**Monthly Process:**
1. **Day 1 (10 min):** Send surveys to all Tier 1 & 2 stakeholders
2. **Day 3 (5 min):** Send reminder to non-responders
3. **Day 5 (15 min):** Close survey, analyze results
4. **Day 6 (30 min):** Follow up on any red flags (scores <3)
5. **Day 7 (10 min):** Update tracking spreadsheet

**Total time investment:** ~70 minutes/month

## Response Rate Strategies

### Get >70% Response Rate:

‚úÖ **Keep it short:** 1 question = 2 minutes max
‚úÖ **Personalize:** Use stakeholder names, not generic "Dear User"
‚úÖ **Timing:** Avoid Monday AM, Friday PM, month-end
‚úÖ **Mobile-friendly:** Test on phone before sending
‚úÖ **Show appreciation:** Thank responders, show you act on feedback
‚úÖ **Close the loop:** Share what you changed based on feedback

‚ùå **Don't do this:**
- Don't send long surveys (kills response rates)
- Don't make it anonymous IF you need to follow up (defeats purpose)
- Don't send surveys too frequently (survey fatigue)
- Don't ignore feedback (destroys trust)

### Segment Your Audience:

**Tier 1 Stakeholders** (High Power, High Interest):
- Survey monthly
- Always follow up on <4 scores
- Personal thank you for responses

**Tier 2 Stakeholders** (Keep Informed):
- Survey monthly
- Follow up on <3 scores
- Automated thank you OK

**Tier 3 Stakeholders** (Monitor):
- Survey quarterly
- Follow up only on <2 scores

## Interpreting Scores

### üü¢ Average >4.0: Excellent Partnership
**What this means:** Stakeholders are highly satisfied with your support.

**What to do:**
- Maintain current approach
- Ask: "What should we keep doing?"
- Share success with CISO and team
- Use as reference for other BISOs

### üü° Average 3.0-4.0: Good, Room for Improvement
**What this means:** Partnership is working, but not exceptional.

**What to do:**
- Review comments for improvement opportunities
- Follow up with lower-scoring stakeholders
- Ask: "What would make this a 5?"
- Address systemic issues (appears in multiple comments)

### üî¥ Average <3.0: Partnership Problems
**What this means:** Stakeholders are neutral to dissatisfied. Immediate action required.

**What to do:**
1. **This week:** Identify root cause (review all comments)
2. **Schedule calls:** 1-on-1s with lowest-scoring stakeholders
3. **Ask directly:** "What's not working? What should I change?"
4. **Create recovery plan:** Specific actions, specific timelines
5. **Check in weekly:** Don't wait for next month's survey
6. **Escalate if needed:** Engage CISO for support

## Red Flag Responses

### When Someone Scores 1-2 (Very Dissatisfied/Dissatisfied)

**Immediate Action (Within 24 hours):**
```
Email template:

Subject: Following up on your feedback

Hi [Name],

Thank you for your honest feedback on this month's pulse survey.
I saw you rated the partnership [score]/5, and I want to understand
what's not working.

Would you have 20 minutes this week for a conversation? I want to
make sure I'm supporting you and your team effectively.

Please let me know a time that works, or I'm happy to work around
your schedule.

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

**During the Call:**
1. **Listen first:** Don't defend, don't explain, just understand
2. **Ask open questions:** "Tell me more about that..." "What would good look like?"
3. **Acknowledge:** "I hear you. That's frustrating."
4. **Commit to action:** "Here's what I'll do..." (Be specific)
5. **Follow up:** "I'll check back in 2 weeks. Here's what you should expect."

## Best Practices

### Do This:
‚úÖ Send surveys same day each month (build routine)
‚úÖ Keep surveys short (1 question + optional comment)
‚úÖ Follow up on low scores within 24 hours
‚úÖ Close the loop (tell stakeholders what you changed)
‚úÖ Track trends over time (more important than single-month scores)
‚úÖ Celebrate improvements (share wins with CISO)

### Don't Do This:
‚ùå Skip months (breaks rhythm, loses trust)
‚ùå Make surveys too long (kills response rates)
‚ùå Ignore negative feedback (destroys credibility)
‚ùå Get defensive about criticism (misses opportunity to improve)
‚ùå Only survey when things are good (defeats early warning purpose)
‚ùå Make it anonymous if you need to follow up (can't address specific issues)

## Common Questions

**Q: Should surveys be anonymous?**
A: No, if you want to follow up on issues. Anonymous surveys don't let you help specific stakeholders. Promise confidentiality instead: "Your individual responses won't be shared, but I will follow up personally to address concerns."

**Q: What if response rates are low?**
A:
1. Shorten the survey (1 question only)
2. Personalize the ask
3. Show how you've acted on previous feedback
4. Try different channels (email vs. Slack)
5. Make it mobile-friendly

**Q: What if scores drop suddenly?**
A: This is the early warning system working!
1. Investigate immediately
2. Check for recent incidents or changes
3. Follow up with stakeholders who scored low
4. Address systemic issues quickly
5. Resurvey in 2-3 weeks to verify improvement

**Q: How do I handle negative comments?**
A: As gifts! Negative feedback is more valuable than positive feedback for improvement. Thank them for honesty, follow up quickly, and show you took action.
