## ğŸ¤ Service 1: Trusted Advisor

### What This Means
BISOs establish partnerships with business leaders, foster trust, and deliver strategic counsel. They act as "mini-CISOs" embedded in your business unit.

### What You Get
- **Strategic Security Guidance** during business planning
- **Early Project Consultation** (before detailed design)
- **Risk-Based Decision Support** with clear tradeoff analysis
- **Business Case Development** with security considerations
- **Executive Communication** that translates security to business language

### When to Engage
- **Monthly:** Business strategy reviews and planning
- **Project Start:** New initiatives (ideation phase)
- **Key Decisions:** Major technology or process changes
- **Quarterly:** Business review for security health

### Real Examples
*"We're considering a mobile app. What security should we include in the business case?"*
*"Board is asking about data protection. Help me prepare the executive summary?"*
*"We want faster time-to-market. How can security enable instead of slow us down?"*

## What BISOs DON'T Do

Setting clear expectations:

âŒ **Implement security controls** (they advise; security/IT teams implement)
âŒ **Approve/deny business decisions** (they advise; business leaders decide)
âŒ **Own security risk** (business units own risk; BISOs help manage)
âŒ **Slow down business** (they enable faster movement with confidence)
âŒ **Replace security teams** (they bridge business and security)
âŒ **Conduct penetration testing** (specialized security teams handle)
âŒ **Manage security incidents** (security operations leads; BISOs support business impact)

## Next Steps for Business Leaders

1. **Identify your assigned BISO** (check with security team)
2. **Schedule introductory meeting** (30 minutes)
3. **Discuss upcoming initiatives** where BISO can add value
4. **Establish regular touchpoints** (monthly or bi-weekly)
5. **Engage early and often** (better outcomes when BISO involved from start)
