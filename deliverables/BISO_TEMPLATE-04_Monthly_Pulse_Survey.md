# BISO Monthly Pulse Survey Template

**Purpose:** Quick monthly assessment of BISO partnership effectiveness and stakeholder satisfaction
**Time to Complete:** 2 minutes for stakeholders, 15 minutes/month to analyze
**Frequency:** Monthly (first week of each month)
**Primary Use:** Early warning system for relationship health and business impact measurement

---

## Survey Overview

This 5-question monthly pulse survey provides rapid feedback on BISO partnership quality, business impact, and stakeholder satisfaction. The survey focuses on **outcomes over activities** â€” measuring whether BISOs enable business success rather than just security compliance.

**Success Threshold:** Average scores >4.0 indicate healthy partnerships; <3.5 requires immediate intervention.

## Collection Guidance

### Who to Survey

**Tier 1 Stakeholders (High Power, High Interest):**
- Survey monthly
- Always follow up on any score <4
- Personal outreach for all responses

**Tier 2 Stakeholders (Keep Informed):**
- Survey monthly
- Follow up on scores <3
- Automated thank you acceptable

**Tier 3 Stakeholders (Monitor):**
- Survey quarterly
- Follow up only on scores <2

**Target Audience:**
- C-Suite executives working with BISOs
- Business unit leaders receiving BISO support
- Project/product managers in active BISO consultations
- Functional leaders (Marketing, Sales, Operations, etc.)

### When to Survey

**Monthly Cadence:**
- **Send:** First Monday of each month
- **Reminder:** Wednesday (if no response)
- **Close:** Friday end of business
- **Analyze:** Following Monday
- **Follow-up:** Within 24-48 hours of identifying issues

**Timing Tips:**
- Avoid month-end (stakeholders too busy)
- Avoid major holidays
- Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons
- Send during business hours (9 AM - 3 PM local time)

### How to Survey

**Recommended Methods:**

1. **Email (Simplest):**
   - Personalized email with questions in body
   - Response via reply or embedded survey link
   - **Best for:** <20 stakeholders

2. **Web Form (Scalable):**
   - Google Forms, SurveyMonkey, Typeform, Microsoft Forms
   - Email with link to form
   - **Best for:** 20-100 stakeholders

3. **Integrated Platform (Enterprise):**
   - Embedded in stakeholder portal
   - Automated monthly reminders
   - **Best for:** 100+ stakeholders, mature programs

**Response Rate Strategies:**
- **Keep it short:** 2 minutes max (emphasize in subject line)
- **Personalize:** Use stakeholder names, reference recent interactions
- **Mobile-friendly:** Test on smartphones
- **Show appreciation:** Thank responders, share how feedback drives improvements
- **Close the loop:** Report back on changes made based on feedback

## Interpretation Guide

### Question 1: Value Assessment (1-5 scale)

**Scoring:**
- **5.0:** Exceptional partnership â€” stakeholder sees BISOs as critical business enablers
- **4.0-4.9:** Strong partnership â€” valuable contributor to business success
- **3.0-3.9:** Adequate partnership â€” meeting basic expectations, room for improvement
- **2.0-2.9:** Weak partnership â€” not meeting stakeholder needs, intervention required
- **1.0-1.9:** Failing partnership â€” immediate escalation and recovery plan needed

**Action Thresholds:**
- ðŸŸ¢ **4.0+:** Maintain current approach, identify best practices to scale
- ðŸŸ¡ **3.0-3.9:** Review comments, schedule follow-up to understand improvement opportunities
- ðŸ”´ **<3.0:** Immediate 1-on-1 call within 24 hours, create recovery plan

### Question 2: Problem vs. Solution Focus

**Scoring:**
- **Solution-focused:** BISOs are helping solve business problems (desired outcome)
- **Problem-focused:** BISOs are only identifying security issues (needs improvement)

**Action Thresholds:**
- ðŸŸ¢ **>75% Solution-focused:** Excellent business integration
- ðŸŸ¡ **50-75% Solution-focused:** Good progress, continue coaching BISOs on solution orientation
- ðŸ”´ **<50% Solution-focused:** Systematic issue â€” review BISO training, consultation approach

**Root Cause Questions:**
- Are BISOs saying "no" without providing alternatives?
- Are BISOs arriving with solutions, not just problems?
- Are BISOs understanding business context before recommending controls?

### Question 3: Responsiveness (1-5 scale)

**Scoring:**
- **5:** Exceptional â€” BISOs respond faster than expected, align with business timelines
- **4:** Fast â€” BISOs meet deadlines consistently
- **3:** Adequate â€” BISOs meet deadlines but with minimal buffer
- **2:** Slow â€” BISOs miss deadlines or require multiple follow-ups
- **1:** Very slow â€” BISOs are bottlenecks to business progress

**Action Thresholds:**
- ðŸŸ¢ **4.0+:** Responsiveness is a strength
- ðŸŸ¡ **3.0-3.9:** Review SLA expectations, identify capacity issues
- ðŸ”´ **<3.0:** Immediate action â€” assess workload, implement response-time SLAs (e.g., 4-hour acknowledgment, 48-hour turnaround)

**Common Solutions:**
- Implement formal SLAs (4-hour response, 2-day turnaround)
- Triage system for urgent vs. routine requests
- Dedicated BISO hours for high-priority stakeholders
- Escalation path for time-sensitive requests

### Question 4: Proactive Involvement

**Scoring:**
- **Yes, absolutely:** Strong indicator of trust and perceived value
- **Maybe, depends:** Conditional trust â€” BISOs valuable in some contexts but not all
- **No, only when required:** Stakeholders see BISOs as compliance burden, not business partner

**Action Thresholds:**
- ðŸŸ¢ **>60% "Yes":** Stakeholders actively seek BISO involvement
- ðŸŸ¡ **40-60% "Yes":** Growing trust, continue demonstrating business value
- ðŸ”´ **<40% "Yes":** Stakeholders avoid BISOs â€” fundamental relationship problem requiring intervention

**Recovery Strategies for "No" Responses:**
1. **Understand why:** Schedule 1-on-1 to learn root cause
2. **Quick wins:** Identify one immediate way to add value
3. **Change approach:** Shift from compliance to enablement messaging
4. **Prove value:** Share case studies of BISO-enabled business wins
5. **Resurvey in 60 days:** Track improvement

### Question 5: Enable vs. Constrain

**Scoring:**
- **Enable:** BISOs accelerate business objectives (desired outcome)
- **Neutral:** BISOs neither help nor hurt business progress
- **Constrain:** BISOs slow down or block business objectives (critical issue)

**Action Thresholds:**
- ðŸŸ¢ **>70% "Enable":** BISOs are business enablers â€” showcase successes
- ðŸŸ¡ **40-70% "Enable":** Mixed results â€” review specific constraints, improve solution offerings
- ðŸ”´ **<40% "Enable" OR any "Constrain":** Critical issue requiring immediate CISO escalation and intervention

**If "Constrain" Response Received:**
1. **Within 24 hours:** Call stakeholder to understand specific constraints
2. **Within 48 hours:** Create action plan to remove blockers
3. **Within 1 week:** Present alternative approaches that enable business objectives
4. **Resurvey in 30 days:** Verify perception has shifted to "Enable" or "Neutral"

## Common Challenges and Solutions

### Challenge: Low Response Rates (<50%)

**Solutions:**
- âœ… **Shorten further:** Remove optional comment field if needed
- âœ… **Executive endorsement:** Have CISO or exec sponsor reinforce importance
- âœ… **Incentivize:** "All respondents entered into raffle" (gift card, lunch)
- âœ… **Change channel:** Try Slack/Teams instead of email
- âœ… **Show impact:** Share: "Last month you said X, here's what we changed"

### Challenge: Consistently Low Scores (Avg <3.5)

**Solutions:**
1. **Root cause analysis:** Schedule focus group with stakeholders to understand systemic issues
2. **BISO training:** Review BISO consultation approach, provide coaching on business enablement
3. **Capacity assessment:** Determine if BISOs are overworked and unable to respond quickly
4. **Expectation reset:** Clarify BISO role and what stakeholders can expect
5. **Escalate:** Engage CISO and executive sponsor for support

### Challenge: "Problem-Focused" Responses Dominating

**Solutions:**
1. **BISO coaching:** Train BISOs to arrive with solutions, not just problems
2. **Consultation framework:** Implement structured consultation process (3 options, recommended approach)
3. **Success stories:** Share examples of BISOs enabling business wins
4. **Stakeholder education:** Clarify that BISOs are business partners, not gatekeepers
5. **Metrics shift:** Reward BISOs for business outcomes, not just risk identification

### Challenge: Negative Comments Without Context

**Solutions:**
1. **Always follow up:** Schedule call to understand specific issues
2. **Ask clarifying questions:** "Can you give me an example?" "What would good look like?"
3. **Listen first:** Don't defend or explain during initial conversation
4. **Commit to action:** "Here's what I'll do..." with specific timeline
5. **Verify improvement:** Resurvey stakeholder in 30 days
